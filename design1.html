Overview

Moongloss was created as a challenge that I gave myself, after searching for the perfect futuristic font for numerical readouts and small sentences of text. It's a display font that has the simplicity of DIN 1451, with the futurism of Eurostile, the legibility of Roboto, and the utility of the 8-segment display on your VCR or microwave.

It’s impossible to find the perfect font with all these characteristics, given that all the desirable typefaces use non-monospaced numbers. That lack of monospacing becomes an issue when trying to align numerical content vertically or use a rapidly changing numerical display, as the numbers tend to jump and shift alignment. Most importantly, I wanted it to look cool but be incredibly legible at any size.

 

Process

I selected a list of characters to be developed (numbers, letters, certain symbols), and shapes for certain characters (e.g. the shape of ‘4’). Lowercase letters were removed from the project scope as I could not maintain my ideal visual consistency with them.

Dimension and style characteristics were selected based on the current trends in futuristic fonts. The initial characters were created in Inkscape as scalable vector graphic images. During initial development I found that fixed width numbers worked well but letters and certain symbols needed to be variable width for visual consistency.

The characters were imported in FontForge for adjustment and compilation into a font file. Adjustments were made for optical perfection, and the width, bearings and kerning were tweaked for visual consistency. Two additional variants of the font were created, having different weights that would increase legibility at different viewing sizes.

The font was tested in-house until it reached the desired level of consistency and perfection. It is available to download on its website or Dafont.
