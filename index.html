My Process

1: Understand

At this stage I want to understand what I’m trying to solve, for whom, why, and what they’d like out of the solution. I talk to the stakeholders and customers to understand their goals and the greater context that surrounds it, as well as their hopes and disappointments. I prefer to use a few open-ended questions that encourage free flowing conversation – these provide many key insights that I can use to create a guiding philosophy for the design process.

2: Inventory/organize/think ahead

Once I have an idea of what I’m designing, who’s going to manage it, and the scope of the project, I begin with a content inventory. This inventory comes from existing content and market/user research and is used to create an information architecture for the project. At this stage, I ensure that the intended information architecture and the technologies used to build the project are compatible. They can both influence, determine, and limit each other, and this can be the most interesting challenge in a project.

 

3: Sketch and prototype

Once the information architecture has been designed, I can begin to test wireframes that present the functionality and interaction of the end product. Successful designs are developed into increasingly detailed prototypes that provide options for fine details such as branding and color.

4: Implement and handover

At this stage high-fidelity prototypes and design specifications are handed over for development. However, good UX has to be maintained long after it was designed. As such, one of my fundamental principles is to provide the client with the necessary guidelines to maintain a consistent UX.
